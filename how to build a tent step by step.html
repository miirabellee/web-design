How to set up a tent

  1. Unpack your tent supplies, A-Frame tents are the most typical and common type of tent for personal use. They're the easiest to erect and usually come with extras like a rain-fly and weather tarp.
  2. Lay down a ground cloth, there may still be twigs and other debris that could puncture your tent.
  3. Insert your tent poles through the frame. In the case of a "tunnel tent", make sure the poles are parallel from one another.
  4. Raise the tent. If the support poles have joints, make them rigid.
  5. Hammer in your tent pegs. This will stabilize the tent and make sure it doesn't move around.
  6. Set up the rain-fly.It's important to keep in mind that tents on their own are usually not waterproof.

Some quick tent definitions

A-Frame tents
a tent supported by a tall frame, giving it nearly perpendicular sides and standing headroom throughout.
Tunnel tent
A double wall tent that is structured to look and acts like a robust tunnel.
Rain-fly
The flysheet of a tent.
